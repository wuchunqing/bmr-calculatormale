What is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate is the minimum energy (kcal) your body needs at complete rest to maintain breathing, circulation, and cell repair. For most men, BMR is 60–75% of total daily burn.

BMRmale = 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age + 5
Mifflin-St Jeor (1990)

BMR vs RMR vs TDEE

  • BMR — strict lab rest, fully fasted
  • RMR — resting rate, often ~10% higher
  • TDEE — BMR × activity (meal planning)
  • Pick activity honestly — most men overestimate

Lean-mass formulas

When body fat % is known (DEXA, BodPod, calipers), lean-mass equations often beat height/weight estimates for trained men.

Katch-McArdle: 370 + 21.6 × Lean kg
Cunningham: 500 + 22 × Lean kg

Which BMR formula is best for men?

A 2005 American Dietetic Association review found Mifflin-St Jeor the most reliable for the general population — within about ±10% for most adults. The male equation adds +5 (women use −161). If you know body fat %, Katch-McArdle often wins for lean or muscular men. Revised Harris-Benedict is useful for comparison but tends to overestimate slightly in modern cohorts. Cunningham is popular with athletes when lean mass is known.

How to use your BMR number

  • Multiply by an activity factor (1.2–1.9) to estimate TDEE — that is your maintenance line.
  • For fat loss, start ~300–500 kcal below TDEE, not at BMR. Stay above ~1,500 kcal/day unless a clinician advises otherwise.
  • For lean bulk, add ~200–300 kcal above TDEE and prioritize protein + progressive lifting.
  • Recalculate after ±5–7 kg weight change, major body-comp shifts, or when progress stalls 2–4 weeks.

FAQ

Which BMR formula is most accurate for men?

Mifflin-St Jeor (1990) is the most widely validated for general populations — typically within ±10% of measured BMR. For lean or muscular men with a reliable body-fat %, Katch-McArdle is often closer. Harris-Benedict tends to overestimate slightly. Lab indirect calorimetry remains the gold standard.

Should I eat at my BMR to lose weight?

No. BMR ignores activity and digestion. Daily targets should start from TDEE (BMR × activity). Eating at or below BMR for long periods is usually too aggressive. A common safety floor for men is about 1,500 kcal/day — discuss aggressive deficits with a clinician.

How is BMR measured in a lab?

Gold standard is indirect calorimetry — measuring O₂ consumption and CO₂ production after overnight fast, 8+ hours sleep, no exercise for ~24 hours, and lying still in a thermoneutral room (about 20–25°C) with an inactive sympathetic nervous system.

What raises or lowers male BMR?

Higher lean mass, larger body size, and healthy thyroid function raise BMR. Age, prolonged severe dieting, poor sleep, low protein, and some illnesses can lower it. Genetics and hormones also matter — two men with the same stats can differ by ~10–15%.

BMR vs TDEE — which do I use for meal planning?

Use TDEE for daily calorie targets. BMR is your resting floor and helps you understand how much activity adds. Activity multipliers already bake in typical NEAT and exercise bands; add awareness of thermic effect of food (~10%) when logging macros.

When should I recalculate BMR?

Recalculate after gaining or losing roughly 10–15 lb (5–7 kg), a clear body-composition change, turning a new decade, or after 12+ weeks of dieting when the scale stalls despite adherence. Treat the number as a starting point — adjust ±100–150 kcal from real-world trend over 2–4 weeks.
